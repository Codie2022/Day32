1.0 Introduction

1.0 Overview

A Daylight Sensor is a device that detects and measures the intensity of ambient light in an environment. These sensors are commonly used in applications like automatic lighting systems, energy-saving controls, and smart homes.

How It Works

Light Detection: The sensor uses a photo-sensitive component such as a photodiode, phototransistor, or Light Dependent Resistor (LDR). These components change their electrical properties based on the amount of light they receive.

Signal Processing: The light intensity is converted into an electrical signal (voltage or current), which is then processed by an electronic circuit or a microcontroller. Output: The sensor outputs data that can control devices (e.g., adjusting the brightness of lights, switching devices on/off) or provide information to a monitoring system.
